# Disaster Relief Standards — Quick Reference
# Source: Based on Sphere Handbook and WHO Emergency Guidelines
# For use in the ReliefRelay RAG corpus

## SECTION 1: TRIAGE CLASSIFICATION

### Medical Urgency Levels

**CRITICAL (RED)**
- Uncontrolled bleeding / hemorrhage
- Unconsciousness or altered mental state
- Severe chest pain (possible cardiac event)
- Severe difficulty breathing / airway obstruction
- Signs of stroke (facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
- Severe burns (>20% body surface area)
- Suspected spinal injury
- Response time: IMMEDIATE — within 15 minutes

**HIGH URGENCY (ORANGE)**
- Open fractures or dislocations
- Head injury with vomiting
- Severe dehydration (especially in elderly or children under 5)
- High fever (>39°C / 102°F) with confusion
- Diabetic emergency
- Medication-dependent conditions without medication (insulin, heart meds)
- Response time: URGENT — within 1-2 hours

**MEDIUM URGENCY (YELLOW)**
- Minor lacerations requiring stitches
- Suspected (not confirmed) fractures
- Moderate dehydration
- Fever (37.5–39°C) without confusion
- Pregnancy (third trimester) without complications
- Mental health crisis without immediate danger
- Response time: TODAY — within 4-6 hours

**LOW PRIORITY (GREEN)**
- Minor cuts and bruises
- Cold/flu symptoms
- Mild dehydration (conscious, no confusion)
- Stable chronic conditions with medication
- General fatigue and stress
- Response time: STANDARD — within 24 hours


## SECTION 2: MINIMUM STANDARDS FOR SHELTER

### Per the Sphere Handbook 2018 — Shelter Standards

**Covered living space:**
- Minimum: 3.5 m² per person in initial emergency
- Target: 4.5–5.5 m² per person for prolonged stays
- Families should not be separated unless by explicit consent

**Thermal comfort:**
- Maintain indoor temperature above 15°C (59°F)
- Provide minimum 1 blanket per person in cool/cold weather
- Priority: infants, elderly, persons with disabilities

**Privacy and safety:**
- Separate toilet facilities by gender
- Lighting required in communal areas and pathways at night
- Special arrangements for persons at risk (unaccompanied minors, SGBV survivors)

**Prioritization (from Sphere Handbook Ch. 2):**
1. Individuals with medical needs
2. Unaccompanied children and separated families
3. Pregnant women and nursing mothers
4. Elderly persons
5. Persons with disabilities


## SECTION 3: WATER AND FOOD STANDARDS

### Water (Sphere Standard 1)
- Minimum: 15 liters per person per day
- Drinking water: 2-3 liters per person per day (more in heat)
- Maximum distance to water point: 500 meters
- Quality: WHO guidelines for drinking water quality

### Food (Sphere Standard 2)
- Minimum caloric intake: 2,100 kcal per person per day
- Priority nutrition support: children under 5, pregnant/breastfeeding women
- Special diets: diabetics, renal disease, severe allergies must be accommodated
- Ready-to-eat foods preferred in first 72 hours


## SECTION 4: REGISTRATION AND DOCUMENTATION

### Case Registration Protocol (UNHCR Emergency Handbook)

**Required fields for intake:**
- Full name (as documented or self-reported)
- Date of birth / approximate age
- Sex / gender
- Nationality and language
- Location where found / last known location
- Family composition (number of people, ages)
- Presenting medical conditions
- Immediate needs (shelter, food, water, medication)
- Contact information if available

**Documentation principles:**
- Consent must be obtained before data collection
- Data should be stored securely (offline-first preferred in disaster zones)
- Vulnerable persons (minors, SGBV survivors) require additional protection protocols
- Sharing case data: only with explicit consent and need-to-know basis


## SECTION 5: SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### Unaccompanied Minors
- Must be referred to child protection officer IMMEDIATELY
- Do not separate siblings
- Assign a dedicated case manager
- Triage level: minimum YELLOW regardless of physical presentation

### Pregnant Women
- In third trimester: triage as minimum YELLOW
- Active labor: triage as CRITICAL (RED) — call emergency services
- Provide access to prenatal care within 24 hours

### Elderly (60+)
- Higher risk of dehydration, hypothermia, medication complications
- Verify medications — many are life-dependent
- Upgrade urgency by one level if isolated or showing confusion

### Persons with Disabilities
- Do not assume needs — ask directly about support requirements
- Ensure accessible shelter assignment
- Mobility aids and equipment should be tracked in case record


## SECTION 6: MENTAL HEALTH FIRST AID

### Psychological First Aid (WHO Guidelines)
- Look: assess for safety, distress, practical needs
- Listen: actively listen without pressuring disclosure
- Link: connect to available services, social support

### Signs requiring urgent mental health referral:
- Active suicidal ideation or self-harm
- Complete inability to care for self or dependents
- Severe dissociation (not recognizing reality)
- Acute psychosis (hallucinations, delusions)

### Do NOT:
- Force people to talk about trauma
- Make promises you cannot keep
- Give false reassurance
- Separate people from their social network


## SECTION 7: ESCALATION CRITERIA

**Escalate to supervisor when:**
- Triage level is RED
- Case involves an unaccompanied minor
- Suspected domestic violence or SGBV
- Person refuses registration but has visible injuries
- Resource shortage prevents meeting minimum standards
- Media or security incident
- Any case where you are unsure

**Contact local emergency services (911 / 999 / 112) when:**
- Life-threatening emergency
- Active violence or threat
- Immediate birth / obstetric emergency
- Suspected poisoning or overdose


## SECTION 8: RESOURCE ALLOCATION PRINCIPLES

### Priority order for limited resources:
1. Critical medical cases (RED)
2. Vulnerable groups (children, pregnant, elderly, disabled)
3. Large family units
4. Single adults with medium/high urgency
5. Stable single adults

### When shelter is at capacity:
- Maintain a waiting list with triage priority
- Coordinate with neighboring facilities
- Set up overflow area (minimum standards still apply)
- Never turn away a RED triage case — activate emergency protocol
